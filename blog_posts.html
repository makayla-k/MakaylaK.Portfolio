What 2022 Has taught me

Friday January 14th, 2023
This past May I graduated with my Bachelors in Computer Science!

It was quite a journey to make it to gradution. Back in 2019 I joined a Computer Science cohort program that basically took over my life for three years. I was taking an average of 18 units a semester, I was cross enrolled between a community college and a CSU, every summer and winter I took classes, I participated in extracurriculars that were apart of the cohorts requirements, and to prepare myself for life after college I did everything I could to make myself a well rounded engineer.

I worked on open source projects, took mobile app development courses, built my own website to show as my portfolio, I even worked part-time as a TA. A month or two before graduation a part of me felt relieved and proud that I could Finally say "I did it!" but then the real worry started to set in. I had no job offers. Unlike my classmates who had previous internship experience and more than likely had a return offer for a big tech company, I did not know where my career even started.

During my last semester of college I worked as a TA for a Non-profit, CodePath. I particularly enjoyed working as a TA because it allowed me to share my knowledge and experience with other students as well as strengthening my skills as an engineer. I was only contracted to work with CodePath till the end of the semester and I knew that I would soon have to find a full time job elsewhere.

I was extremely worried and overwhelmed. I worked so hard to get my degree and it still felt like it wasn't enough. I was completely filled with imposter syndrome that I wouldn't be given an opportunity after college. But little did I know that CodePath's TA position would open huge doors for me!

I was contracted to work with META! Being blessed with this opportunity gave me the confidence that I am a capable engineer! Being a woman and a minority in tech it is so easy to face imposter syndrome and have doubts. I remember constantly comparing myself to my male classmates but the thing I had to remember was that even though I may work and process things differently from others, it does not mean that my skills do not have value.

If I could go back to and tell myself one thing it would be to take advantage of every opportunity that comes your way and never belittle yourself. This was just the beginning of my career and I am forever grateful for this opportunity!
